line=1231 tag=M:     Though there is no given word class that makes a three-way distinction, the fact that pronominals cut
line=1232 tag=M:  the A / S / O space one way (A&S vs O) while nominals cut it the other way (A vs S&O) allows the
line=1233 tag=M:  establishment of a tripartite system for Wambaya, with ergative/locative, nominative and accusative
line=1234 tag=M:  cases distinguished (Nordlinger 1998). In this example and others like it, it so happens that both the S
line=1235 tag=M:  and the O argument have the same zero form, but replacing them with prominals would allow us to see
line=1236 tag=M:  that with the right exponents the two cases can be distinguished.

line=1241 tag=M:  specific, however, whereas the reflexive/reciprocal construction described above is possible with all
